About Surabhi Gir Goshala

Nestled in the tranquil environs of Raghunathapura village surrounded by lush mango & palm groves, lies the Surabhi Gir Goshala – a sanctuary dedicated to the sacred service of the Mother cow (Gomata). It's more than just a shelter, it is a spiritual heaven where compassion, devotion & community converge. We believe cow is a sacred mirror to the cosmos, reflecting purity, peace & selfless giving. It is a living tribute to the ancient wisdom. Every act of care is a prayer, provide a loving house where these gentle souls live with dignity, surrounded by nature & nurtured with devotions.

OUR MISSION

Compassion in Daily Action

We exist to rescue, nourish, and rehabilitate cows with dignity. Every routine — from fodder prep to bedtime checks — is designed to keep our herd calm, healthy, and loved.

Go-Seva (Service of Mother Cow)

Verse: Namo brahmanya-devaya go-brahmana hitaya ca, Jagad-dhitaya krisnaya govidaya namo namaha

Meaning: "I offer my respectful obeisances unto Lord Krishna who is the worshipable deity for all brahmanas, the well wisher of the cows and the brahmanas and the benefactor of the whole world. I offer my repeated obeisances to the personality of godhead, known as Krishna and Govinda."

Vishnu purana (1.19.65)

Verse: Surye tu vidyaya trayya havisagnu yajeta mam, atithyena tu vipragrye gosv anga yavasadina

Meaning: Krishna tells Uddhava, "My dearest Uddhava, I can be worshipped within the cows by offerings of grass and other suitable grains and paraphernalia for the pleasure and health of the cows."

Shrimad Bhagavatam (11.11.43-45)

Annadāna

Verse: Yagna-sistasinah santo mucyante sarva-kilbisaih, Bhunjate te tv agham papa ye pacanty atma-karanat

Meaning: "The devotees of the lord released from all kinds of sins because they eat food which is offered first for sacrifice. Others, who prepare food for personal sense of enjoyment, verify eat only sin."

Bagavat-gita (3.13)

Holy Name

Verse: Maharsinam bhrgur aham giram asmy ekam aksaram yajnanam japa-yagnosmi

Meaning: Krishna says, "I am the chanting of holy names (japa, bhajans)"

Our Three Core Objectives

1. To Protect and Nurture Gomata

To serve and safeguard our cows, involving the public in this sacred duty of caring for Mother Cow.

2. To Distribute Sanctified Prasadam

To nourish the community villages both spiritually and physically through the distribution of sattvic and sanctified food.

3. To Spread the Holy Name

To distribute spiritual merit and happiness to all through joyous and uplifting practice of Harinama Sankirtana (Bhajans).

Our Journey of Growth & Devotion

Milestones in Our Sacred Mission

February 2018

Arrival of First Beloved Cows

Saraswathi and Kumkum became the first gentle souls to grace our goshala, marking the beginning of our sacred journey.

February 2019

Inaugural Pooja and Sankirtan

We celebrated the official opening of our goshala with inaugural pooja and began our regular bhajan sessions.

February 2024

Deity Installation

Pranapratishthana (deity installation) of Sri Sri Parama Karuna Nitai Gauranga and Sri Sita Rama temple, bringing divine presence to our sanctuary.

Ongoing

Community Seva

We regularly conduct pooja and bhajans, distributing 500 servings of nutritious blessed food across 18 villages, spreading nourishment and joy throughout the community.

Our Daily Rhythm

A typical day begins with mangala aarti, warm water sponge baths, and fresh-cut fodder. Afternoons are for physiotherapy walks, shade rotations, and hoof care. Nights end with soothing chants and final patrols.
